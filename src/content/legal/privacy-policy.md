# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 15th June, 2026** · Version 1.0

This Privacy Policy explains how **KHIJANI COGNITIVE SYSTEMS LIMITED** ("Khijani", "we", "us",
"our") collects, uses, shares, and protects personal data across the Khijani
platform — our marketing website, our teacher and administrator
portal, and our student app (together, the "Platform").

We are committed to handling personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently in
accordance with **Ghana's Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)** and the applicable
data protection laws of the other African countries in which participating schools
operate.

> **If you are a parent or guardian of a student:** please also read our short,
> plain-language [Student & Parent Privacy Notice](student-parent-privacy-notice.md),
> which describes exactly what we collect about students and your rights.

**Our role depends on whose data it is:**

- **For student personal data**, the **school** that enrols the student is the
  **data controller**. Khijani acts as the school's **data processor** and only
  processes student data on the school's documented instructions.
- **For teacher, administrator, school-contact, and website-visitor data**, and
  for our own business and product-improvement purposes, Khijani is the **data
  controller**.

This Policy covers Khijani's practices in both roles. Where a school is the
controller, that school's own privacy notice also applies to its students.

## 2. Whose data we process

- **Website visitors** — anyone who visits [WEBSITE URL].
- **School contacts and prospective customers** — people who enquire about or
  purchase Khijani for a school.
- **Teachers and administrators** — staff who hold portal accounts.
- **Students** — learners enrolled by a school to use the app (see role note above
  and the Parent Notice).

## 3. What we collect

### 3.1 Website visitors

- Information you submit through enquiry forms or WhatsApp links (e.g. your name,
  school, message).
- Technical and usage data: IP address, browser/device type, pages viewed,
  referring page, and similar analytics data collected via cookies and similar
  technologies (see our [Cookie Policy](cookie-policy.md)).

### 3.2 Teachers and administrators

- Account data: full name, email address, role, and the school/classes you are
  linked to.
- Authentication data managed by our infrastructure provider.
- Usage data: actions taken in the portal, session windows you open, and support
  correspondence.

### 3.3 Students (processed on behalf of the school)

We collect the minimum needed to run the learning activities and produce the
Khijani Index:

- **Identity:** full name, class, and year group, and a non-secret join **code**
  (students do **not** provide an email address or password).
- **Learning activity:** answers and free-text reasoning a student writes during
  conundrums and games (e.g. positions chosen, pre/post reasoning, perspective
  answers, reflections), selections made, and outcomes.
- **Derived scores:** dimension scores and the overall **Khijani Index**, which is
  a pedagogical indicator of critical-thinking development — **not** a clinical,
  psychometric, medical, or IQ assessment.

We do **not** ask students for sensitive personal data, and we ask schools and
teachers not to enter sensitive personal data (such as health, religion, or
ethnicity) into free-text fields.

### 3.4 School contacts / billing

- Contact details for the people who manage the school relationship.
- Billing and invoicing details (billing is handled offline by invoice / bank
  transfer; we do **not** collect or store card numbers).

## 4. Why we process data, and our lawful basis

Under Act 843 and comparable laws, we rely on the following bases:

| Purpose | Lawful basis |
|---|---|
| Providing portal/app accounts and running learning activities | Performance of a contract (with the school); the school's instructions |
| Producing and displaying the Khijani Index and analytics to teachers | The school's instructions as controller; legitimate educational interest |
| Processing student data | On documented instruction of the school (controller), which is responsible for the parental-consent basis |
| Responding to enquiries and providing support | Legitimate interests; steps to enter a contract |
| Securing the Platform and preventing abuse | Legitimate interests; legal obligation |
| Improving and developing the Platform | Legitimate interests, using aggregated/de-identified data where possible |
| Sending service and (where permitted) marketing communications to school staff | Legitimate interests / consent, with an opt-out |
| Meeting legal, accounting, and regulatory obligations | Legal obligation |

We do not sell personal data, and we do not use student data for advertising or to
build commercial profiles.

## 5. How we share data

We share personal data only as needed:

- **With the school** that controls the relevant student data.
- **With trusted service providers** who help us host and operate the Platform
  under contract and only on our instructions.
- **For legal reasons** — where required by law, regulation, court order, or a
  lawful request from a competent authority.
- **In a business transfer** — if Khijani is involved in a merger, acquisition, or
  asset sale, subject to the protections in this Policy.

We never make student personal data available to third parties for their own
marketing.

## 6. International transfers

Our infrastructure providers may process or store data on servers located outside
your country. Where personal data is transferred across borders, we take steps
required by applicable law to ensure an adequate level of protection — including
contractual safeguards with our providers and reliance on recognised transfer
mechanisms. Schools may request details of where their data is hosted.

## 7. How long we keep data

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes above:

- **Student and learning data:** for the duration of the school's subscription and
  then for up to **12 months**, after which it is deleted or anonymised,
  unless the school instructs earlier deletion or longer retention is required by
  law.
- **Teacher/admin accounts:** while the account is active, then deleted or
  anonymised within a reasonable period.
- **Enquiry and billing records:** as required for legitimate business and legal/
  accounting purposes.

On termination of a school's subscription, we delete or return student data in
line with our agreement with the school.

## 8. How we protect data

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures, including encryption in
transit, access controls and role-based permissions, row-level security on our
database, audit logging, and provider-level safeguards. No system is perfectly
secure, but we work to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or
misuse, and we maintain a process for handling data-breach incidents in line with
Act 843.

## 9. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

- **access** the personal data we hold about you;
- **correct** inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **request deletion** of your data;
- **object to or restrict** certain processing;
- **withdraw consent** where we rely on it; and
- **complain** to a data protection authority.

**How to exercise your rights:**

- **Students / parents:** contact your **school** first — the school is the
  controller and can action most requests directly. The school may pass the request
  to us as its processor.
- **Teachers, admins, website visitors, school contacts:** contact us at
  **[PRIVACY EMAIL]**. We will respond within the timeframe required by law.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the **Data Protection Commission
of Ghana** (or the relevant authority in your country).

## 10. Children's data

Khijani is designed for use by students within a school setting. Students are
enrolled by their school, and we process their data only as the school's processor.
**The school is responsible for establishing the appropriate consent basis,
including obtaining parental or guardian consent where required.** We provide the
[Student & Parent Privacy Notice](student-parent-privacy-notice.md) for schools to
share with parents. We do not knowingly allow children to create accounts directly
or provide personal data to us outside the school-enrolled context.
